Product thesis

A cursor-native conversion layer for apparel product pages.

The prototype watches shopping behavior directly on the PDP: size hesitation, image interest, delivery checking, quantity changes, review reading, and time-on-page. It responds at the exact point of uncertainty with a small cursor-side assistant instead of a generic chatbot.

Most shoppers do not ask. They hesitate.

A shopper might never open a chat bubble, but they will inspect sizes, revisit images, read reviews, check delivery, change quantity, and sit on the page before deciding. Those behaviors are live intent.

size
Fit uncertaintyRepeated size activity means the shopper may need sizing confidence.
image
Visual intentImage focus can become a virtual try-on prompt, not a fit prompt.
dwell
Purchase readinessLong page time can trigger a checkout nudge or coupon.

The PDP is a map of intent signals.

We do not need screen vision for the first version. DOM events tell us enough: size hovers, image interest, delivery checks, quantity changes, review reading, and product dwell.

size
Repeated size eventsHover XS, click M, open Size Guide. Trigger fit help only inside this area.
try-on
Image focusHover or click product images. Ask if they want to see the item on themselves.
ship
Delivery uncertaintyPrompt for pincode when the shopper checks delivery or returns.
time
30-second product dwellIf the shopper stays, offer a light discount nudge with XYZ10.
qty
Quantity changeIf they move to two pieces, offer a second-item discount.

Each intervention belongs to one zone.

The assistant should feel like a retail associate who knows when to speak softly. It should not interrupt every click, image view, or scroll. The message must match the part of the page the shopper is using.

fit
"Need fit help?"Appears only after repeated behavior around size selectors or the size guide.
try-on
"Want to see it on yourself?"Appears around product images, then opens upload-based try-on.
offer
"Take XYZ10 for 10% off."Appears after 30 seconds on the page, then Space adds the product to bag.
ship
"Check delivery timing?"Appears around delivery/returns and asks for pincode.
review
"Want the review pattern?"Summarizes common fit complaints and quality signals.

Scope it as a sequence of buying moments.

The first version should prove that the assistant can wait, notice, and intervene with the right action. From there, expand by adding one more shopper uncertainty at a time.

Size hesitation

The shopper hovers sizes, clicks M, returns to Size Guide, and pauses. The assistant types "Need fit help?" near the cursor.

Example 02

Product dwell

The shopper spends 30 seconds on the PDP. The assistant offers XYZ10 and lets Space add the item to bag.

Example 03

Virtual try-on

The shopper studies product images. The assistant asks if they want to see it on themselves and opens an upload panel.

Example 04

Delivery timing

The shopper checks delivery or returns. The assistant asks for pincode and estimates when the product will arrive.

Example 05

Review summary

The shopper lingers near reviews. The assistant summarizes the pattern: sleeves run snug, fabric feels soft, neckline is liked.

Example 06

Quantity upsell

The shopper increases quantity. The assistant offers a small second-piece discount to move the basket value up.
